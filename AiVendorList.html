Organizations Providing AI and Related Tools for Clinical Research

Site best viewed on a large display


The list below is provided to illustrate what types of tools are currently available. This list is not comprehensive, and no endorsement is intended.

Apple Apple is transforming clinical research and healthcare in a variety of ways. The "ResearchKit" allows clinical researchers to engage with Apple's technology.
  • Evaluation of the Use of Apple Watch Features for Identification of Cardiac Arrhythmias
  • What Apple's Health Records update means for the clinical trials industry
  • The apple research kit: what does it mean for clinical trials
  • GSK starts trial with apple researchkit
  • Novartis uses researchkit
Converseon Converseon provides a semi-supervised ML interface to process social listening data, which currently is used more in marketing than in clinical research. However, their technology can be adapted for clinical trial use.
  • Five benefits of social listening for clinical trials.
  • A clinical trial testing whether social listening can help recruitment in clinical trials
  • Top 17 Social Media Monitoring Vendors
Datavant Datavant aggregates and structures healthcare data to generate actionable insights for clinical trials. It's part of the so called "Vants", founded quite recently, with a disruptive and innovative focus, but still unproven record.
  • Parexel and Datavant Partner to Deliver Advanced Data and Real-World Evidence Analytics to Enhance Drug Development and Commercialization
  • Silicon Valley vet to run new Roivant trial AI company
  • A Story About Roivant, Axovant, and Myovant
  • Datavant Selects the Duke Clinical Research Institute as Founding Academic Partner to Accelerate Innovation in Data-Driven Clinical Research
Deep6 Deep6 applies natural language processing (a type of AI) on to unstructured electronic health records to find patients who may be eligible for clinical trials.
  • TD2 and Deep 6 AI Partner to Accelerate Oncology Clinical Trial Recruitment
  • Cedars Sinai taps AI to pinpoint patients for clinical trials

  • Patient reported

Facebook In the news lately for the Cambridge Analytica scandal, facebook has made efforts to enable their platform for clinical trial use, and has reached out to researchers.
  • Facebook held a special breakfast for drug marketers about recruiting people for clinical trials
  • Facebook offers untapped potential in clinical trial recruitment
Fitbit The bulky 'actigraphs' of the past are still used, but Fitbit is doing its part to change that. Its a competitive space, with other players developing wearables of various sorts for applications in clinical research. Due to this competition, Fitbit's research capabilities are increasing swiftly.
  • Google, Fitbit ink collab healthcare deal, look to challenge Apple Watch
  • Monitoring Physical Activity (Fitbit)
  • 21 clinical trials that are using Fitbit activity trackers right now
Google Like other tech giants, Google has also begun to dabble in the clinical research arena. Adwords is becoming a solid option to consider as part of a clinical trial recruitment strategy, but Google is doing other things too.

Ibm Another giant that has made a commitment towards healthcare and clinical research. The Watson AI platform provides a number of features that are applicable to different phases of a clinical trial.
  • Mayo Clinic, IBM's Watson tackle clinical trial research
  • IBM Watson for Clinical Trial Matching
  • 4 reasons MD Anderson put IBM Watson on hold
Iqvia By merging, IMS and Quintiles combined expertise sets in datascience and clinical trial operations, and now can do some things not possible for other organizations.
  • Patients are talking about their health. Can you hear them?
  • Big Data & Predictive Analytics
Medable With a surfeit of firms, apps and an evermore complex technology environment, integrators can serve a need for some organizations. This small firm attempts to do that, and is establishing a presence in moble data capture.
  • Medable launches Axon, enabling researchers to build ResearchKit apps without developers
  • Medable Launches Cloud-Based Machine Learning Solution for Healthcare
Mindstronghealth Mindstrong health, as the name suggests, focuses on neuropsychiatric disorders that has developed digital phenotypes based on data captured from devices.
  • Mindstrong Health Identifies Digital Biomarkers of Cognitive Function in NPJ Digital Medicine Study
  • Mindstrong Health and Takeda Partner to Explore Development of Digital Biomarkers for Mental Health Conditions
Oracle Oracle can be considered a workhorse in some ways, powering many processes in the background, and like many others is venturing into the cloud computing arena. It would be interesting to see if Oracle can leverage its legacy to become a major data aggregator for clinical development, as trials transition more and more to newer forms of data.
  • Short video about Oracle Health Sciences
  • Oracle Clinical youtube presentation
  • Oracle Clinical: Two Customers Speak
Science37 While many firms provide mobile solutions, Science37 provides a nearly complete package that can be used to conduct a siteless trial.
  • Virtual drug trials boost results
  • UCB collaborates with Science 37 on virtual clinical trials
  • Novartis goes digital with FocalView for ophthalmology clinical trials
Threadresearch Thread offers e-platform, cloud and telehealth, smartphone apps, ePROs, and other services. Its suite of offerings can support many aspects of siteless trials.
  • Medidata & THREAD Enable Remote Patient Research
  • THREAD Research Launches UCSF PRIDE Study iPhone App to Study LGBTQ Health
Transcelerate TransCelerate fosters collaborations between pharma companies and other organizations, and develops frameworks and systems to address needs in clinical research. It provides templates and tools on its website listed as "Initiative Assets" (about half-way down the website linked to on the left).
  • TransCelerate Launches New Initiatives in Automation, Analytics and Data Quality
  • TransCelerate BioPharma Grows Industry Collaboration
  • TransCelerate Establishes Technology Common Protocol Template and Collaborates with the FDA/NIH
Trinetx Less than 10 years ago, Trinetx started as a venture fund organization that, using proprietary technology that interacted with medical records, specialized in helping sponsors and sites identify patients likely to meet protocol entry criteria. Its service offerings have since branched quite considerably.
  • TriNetX teams up with Sanofi, Cornell for better clinical trial design
  • TriNetX Unveils Natural Language Processing to Improve Protocol Design
  • Celgene teams up with TriNetX for next-gen drug trial design
Twitter Twitter allows for very rapid dissemination of information and can be part of a social media campaign for clinical trial recruitment.
  • Twitter as a Potential Data Source for Cardiovascular Disease Research
  • Clinical Trial Recruitment Using Social Media is Growing
Vivosense Vivosense (previously known as Vivonoetics) is a small organization that specializes in algorithms that analyze the new types of data available from wearables and sensors. A nice feature of their website is that it provides some information on specific case studies.
  • ActiGraph Partners with VivoSense to Enhance Secure Access to Regulated Sensor Data from Patients in Pharmaceutical Clinical Trials
  • Getting Started with VivoSense
  • Roche selects Vivonoetics to provide advanced respiratory assessment
