In the past, ML capabilities were available to primarily to large private, government and academic institutions, but are now becoming widely available (although big organizations maintain many advantages). Many organizations are providing standard platforms and (often free) training, e.g. Google, Amazon, Hortonworks and others.



MLaaS

Machine Learning as a Service, or "MLaaS" is becoming a term of use in the trade, and refers to the off-the-shelf (often in a cloud-computing format) provision of tools such data visualization, facial recognition, natural language processing, predictive analytics and deep learning. The number of providers making standard and customized "APIs" (application program interfaces) that allow machine learning to be conveniently applied to business opportunities is increasing rapidly.



Many vendors and organizations provide MLaaS and related services. Formation of partnerships and collaborations is quite frequent (between tech and tech, tech and CROs, etc.).

    Artificial Intelligence

  • "The capability of a machine to imitate intelligent human behavior" - (Merriam Webster)
  • "Artificial intelligence (AI) makes it possible for machines to learn from experience, adjust to new inputs and perform human-like tasks" - (SAS)
  • "The theory and development of computer systems able to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence, such as visual perception, speech recognition, decision-making, and translation between languages" - (Google Dictionary)


    • Machine Learning

  • "A computer program is said to learn from experience E with respect to some class of tasks T and performance measure P if its performance at tasks in T, as measured by P, improves with experience E" - (Tom Mitchell, 1997)
  • "A branch of artificial intelligence based on the idea that systems can learn from data, identify patterns and make decisions with minimal human intervention." - (SAS)



    • Big Data

  • "Data that provides an interesting challenge for computer systems: data sets are generally quite large, taxing the capacities of main memory, local disk, and even remote disk. We call this the problem of big data" - (NASA 1997)
  • "The ability of society to harness information in novel ways to produce useful insights or goods and services of significant value" and "things one can do at a large scale that cannot be done at a smaller one, to extract new insights or create new forms of value" - (Viktor Mayer-Schönberger and Kenneth Cukier, 2013)


    • Unstructured Data

  • "Unstructured data represents any data that does not have a recognizable structure" and "instances of unstructured textual data include Word documents, PowerPoint presentations, instant messages, collaboration software, documents, books, social media posts and medical records. Non-textual unstructured data is generally created in media, such as MP3 audio files, JPEG images and Flash video files" - (Techopedia)



    • Structured Data

  • "Data that resides in a fixed field within a record or file. This includes data contained in relational databases and spreadsheet" - (Webopedia)



    • Supervised Learning

  • "The process of teaching a model by feeding it input data as well as correct output data. This input/output pair is usually referred to as "labeled data" - (Deepai.org)


    • Unsupervised Learning

  • Type of machine learning algorithm used to draw inferences from datasets consisting of input data without labeled responses - (Mathworks)


    • Reinforcement Learning

  • "Reinforcement learning is the problem of getting an agent to act in the world so as to maximize its rewards" - (UBC)


    • Natural Language Processing

  • "Branch of artificial intelligence that helps computers understand, interpret and manipulate human language" - (SAS)


    • Digital Exhaust

  • The concept overlaps to some extent with the concepts of unstrutured data and big data, but generally refers to the informational by-product that constantly streams from web apps, mobile devices, POS systems, and other sources we interact with" (storagecraft.com).
  • Medical Data in particular, with the widespread adoption of EHRs, and with the digitization of laboratory, electrocardiographic, radiographic and other forms of data has generated a tremendous amount of digital exhaust. Making sense of this data is theoretically possible, has tremendous potential and wide-ranging implications, but there are a number of things to consider, as this short linked article discusses.


    • Isolation and Segregation of Data

  • Data isolation and data segregation are sometimes important and necessary to ensure security and protect privacy, but there is a trade-off because isolation may reduce opportunities for models to learn
  • Note this meaning of segregation/integration differs from the concept of multimodal data fusion in that the former refers to the extent of barriers between different sources (or means of production) of data, whereas the latter refers to approaches and techniques to meaningful merge data of varying content coming in diverse formats and structures


    • Multimodal Data Fusion

  • From the linked article: "The well-accepted paradigm that certain natural processes and phenomena can express themselves under completely different physical guises is the raison d etre of multimodal data fusion. Too often, however, very little is known about the underlying relationships among these modalities. Therefore, the most obvious and essential endeavour to be undertaken in any multimodal data analysis task is exploratory: to learn about relationships between modalities, their complementarity".
  • This recent article in Nature discusses multimodal data fusion and other topics including several recommendations for different stake-holders, and provides a useful glossary as a supplement.


    • Data Lake

  • "A data lake is a centralized repository that allows you to store all your structured and unstructured data at any scale. You can store your data as-is, without having to first structure the data, and run different types of analytics—from dashboards and visualizations to big data processing, real-time analytics, and machine learning to guide better decisions" (aws.Amazon.com)
