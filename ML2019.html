This video is one the best illustrations available currently on the internet, which in a few minutes provides an introduction to the 'black box' that machine learning is often perceived as.

In the past, ML capabilities were available to primarily to large private, government and academic institutions, but are now becoming widely available (although big organizations maintain many advantages). Many organizations are providing standard platforms, 'apps', and training, e.g. Google, Amazon, Hortonworks and others.



MLaaS

"Machine Learning as a Service, or "MLaaS"" is becoming a term of use in the trade, and refers to the provision of tools including data visualization, face recognition, natural language processing, predictive analytics and deep learning. The number of providers making standard and customized "APIs" (application program interfaces) that allow machine learning to be conveniently applied to business opportunities is increasing rapidly.
